# Nuclear AI Infrastructure Boi

## Thesis Boi

Nuclear is not just an energy trade here.
It is an AI infrastructure trade built around firm 24/7 power, grid scarcity, and the market’s need for baseload electricity that can support data-center growth.

The core idea:
- AI workloads need uninterrupted power
- grid interconnection is slow
- utilities and reactors with firm generation gain strategic value
- uranium supply tightness adds a second-order squeeze to the theme

## Ticker Boi

Primary names:
- SMR - NuScale Power
- OKLO - Oklo
- VST - Vistra
- CCJ - Cameco
- CEG - Constellation Energy

Role map:
- SMR: small modular reactor optionality
- OKLO: advanced nuclear / reactor development optionality
- VST: existing power generation and data-center power beneficiary
- CEG: high-quality nuclear utility / baseload beneficiary
- CCJ: uranium supply chain / fuel leverage

## Macro Signal Boi

The main macro signals are:
- AI data-center power demand is accelerating
- 24/7 baseload power is becoming a strategic bottleneck
- grid interconnection delays are pushing buyers toward direct power solutions
- hyperscalers are signing or exploring nuclear-linked agreements
- uranium supply remains constrained relative to future demand
- policy support for nuclear is improving as energy security becomes a bigger issue

The market is starting to price nuclear less like a legacy utility and more like an enabling layer for AI capacity buildout.

## Company Boi

SMR and OKLO are the most speculative names in the basket.
They offer the biggest upside if SMR commercialization becomes real, but they also carry the highest execution risk.

VST and CEG are the cleaner institutional expressions.
They have operating fleets, clearer cash flow visibility, and better fit with a quality-first framework.

CCJ is the fuel-chain lever.
It does not need reactor commercialization to work, but it does need sustained uranium demand and disciplined contracting.

## Risk Boi

Main risks:
- commercialization timing on SMR / OKLO
- regulatory and permitting delays
- cost overruns and project execution risk
- nuclear sentiment can move faster than actual deployment
- utilities can re-rate without giving the full optionality to the reactor developers

## Signal Quality Boi

- CEG and VST for operating power exposure

Lower signal quality:
- pure narrative trades without a contract, shipment, or licensing milestone
